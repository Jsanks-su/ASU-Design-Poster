# Color System

Color is narrative structure, not surface styling. Every poster prompt must specify a color strategy.

## Color Decision Sequence

1. Define emotional temperature: cold, warm, feverish, ceremonial, clinical, dusty, tender, violent, nostalgic, uncanny.
2. Choose color stance: congruent by default, or counterpoint, bold accent, popular-readable, material-historical, painterly, or monochrome when that better serves the poster idea.
3. Set palette roles: dominant field, supporting colors, rupture/accent color, text color, material surface.
4. Set behavior: saturation, contrast, lighting source, ink/paint/print density, paper or film stock.

## Color Stances

| Stance | Use when | Palette logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Congruent | The content has a natural color world. | Let subject and genre lead: forest blacks/greens, Song ink/stone/paper, noir black/red, courtroom wood/cream/dark suit. |
| Counterpoint | Color should create tension against the subject. Use only when the contrast reveals a stronger point than expected color. | Soft pastel for cruelty, clean white for corruption, festive red for grief, toy-like brightness for menace, warm home light for horror. |
| Bold accent | One color should become the memory hook. | Mostly restrained palette with a single rupture: red slash, green sprout, yellow evidence tag, blue screen glow. |
| Popular-readable | The image must scan fast and appeal broadly. | Clear contrast, simple dominant hue, high legibility, controlled complementary color, no muddy prestige palette. |
| Material-historical | The surface carries period or evidence. | Rice paper, oxidized red seal, ink black, ash gray, sepia archive, cyanotype, newsprint, faded celluloid. |
| Painterly | A painting lineage guides color construction. | Chiaroscuro dark/light, impressionist broken color, expressionist unnatural color, fauvist saturation, fresco earth pigments. |
| Monochrome | Shape, type, or symbol should dominate. | Black/white/cream with one accent; blue-gray field; charcoal paper; red-only rupture; ink density as depth. |

## Palette Moves

- Black / cream / red: revenge, legal severity, graphic violence, constructivist urgency.
- Ink black / rice paper / oxidized seal red: Chinese historical, archive, judgment, memory.
- Forest black / ash gray / leaf green: ecology, extinction, renewal against damage.
- Clinical white / surgical green / warning red: medical, forensic, institutional dread.
- Sodium orange / cyan night / wet black: urban noir, loneliness, pursuit.
- Dust gold / stone gray / smoke brown: historical epic, ruins, burden, old power.
- Candy pastel / hidden black: counterpoint for menace, satire, childhood corrupted.
- Deep blue / small warm light: awe, distance, longing, sci-fi human scale.
- Flat primary blocks: modernist, pop, public campaign, bold commercial recognition.
- Desaturated skin / one impossible color: social thriller, psychological break.

## Prompt Requirements

Avoid generic phrases such as "beautiful cinematic colors" or "rich palette." Specify:

- dominant colors
- accent color and its narrative role
- saturation level
- contrast level
- light source or print material
- text/background contrast

Example:

```text
Color strategy: ash-gray destroyed forest and matte black bear silhouette, with one small living leaf green accent as the only saturated color; cream paper background, high contrast title in weathered black.
```

## Anti-Low Color

Avoid:

- default teal-orange unless genre-specific
- automatic purple-blue cyberpunk
- all-dark prestige sameness
- random rainbow gradients
- gold luxury templates
- neon accents with no story role
- muddy monochrome where the subject disappears

Repair by assigning one color job: evidence, wound, hope, corruption, memory, signal, ritual, or danger.

Counterpoint color must name both sides of the contrast, for example "gentle festival red used against a revenge story to make violence feel ritualized." If it cannot be explained that clearly, use a congruent or bold-accent palette.
